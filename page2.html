SOURCES

I am working with two datasets.

The first, comes courtesy of the UCSB Cylinder Audio Archive.  It contains 7318 rows and 25 columns. Its categories include:  Primary Title, Description, Main Talent, and Type. 

The dataset affords us the opportunity to trace different U.S. audio histories, including the history of music. My focus here, however, will be on spoken word recordings - as the origin story for audiobooks.

Below is an image of the UCSB Cylinder dataset.

The second database comes courtesy of the American Publishers Asssociation. It contains 2550 rows and 8 columns of data on Audie Award winners and nominees since the Audies were first held in 1996. 

        "An Audie Award (or Audie), bestowed annually in the United States, recognizes         outstanding audiobooks and spoken-word entertainment. The Audies have been granted         by the Audio Publishers Association, the not-for-profit trade organization of the audiobook         industry, since 1996." - Wikipedia
